My Story

When I was in high school, I won a gold medal in the National Biology Olympiad and received direct admission to Tsinghua University. Now I am a senior undergraduate student at the School of Life Sciences. During my undergraduate studies, my research focus has been on Neurobiology and Synthetic Biology. In my first year, I was selected for the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) team, which usually picks second-year students. With Prof. Guoqiang Chen's guidance, I spent a year researching Synthetic Biology. From my second year, I joined Prof. Yi Zhong's lab, studying the mechanisms of learning and memory. During the summer of my third year, I worked at the Hospital for Sick Children (SickKids) under the mentorship of Prof. Sheena Josselyn, who is one of the early pioneers of the engram theory. This experience helped me dive deeper into learning and memory research and introduced me to new techniques.
Outside of academics, music holds a place in my heart. I've been learning opera singing (bel canto) since my freshman year and have joined marching band, choir as well as musical club, and have won Expressive Musical Genius & Gold Prize (Age 18-21) in Global Genius Music Competition and Dazzling Virtuoso Special Award & Gold Prize (Romantic B) in Royale Music Competition. I genuinely believe that science and art enrich one another, highlighting the diverse ways humans can be creative. In short, I am someone with a profound appreciation for both the intricacies of the brain and the magic of music, striving to become a scientist with refined tastes and aspirations.

Projects

Finding the Neural Basis of Memory Integration in Rodents

I applied Python DABEST package for data analysis on the duration of time mice spent in different zones of an open arena, refining the analytical process and optimizing output results. And I developed a new data visualization method based on Matlab polarscatter function, adding time information to existing orientation angles of mice using color and radius. Generated figures for potential publication. To gain a deeper understanding of the experimental paradigms, I also engaged in Morris water maze experiments on mice to explore the impacts of social isolation on spatial memory.
This work is in preparation, will be submitted to Cell (Wang et al., 2023 [Unpublished]).

Characterization of Positive and Negative Engrams with MAPseq in the Lateral Amygdala

Conducted design and implementation of molecular cloning to integrate optogenetics into MAPseq, complemented by performing immunohistochemistry to validate the efficacy of viral injections. In addition, I utilized the "ezTrack" pipeline developed by Cai lab to score the freezing behavior of mice, and performed linear regression with the manual scoring results to verify its effectiveness.

Memory Retrieval Induces Cross-modal Updating of Multi-modal Memory and Its Underlying Mechanisms

I independently formulated the research question, designed and executed a comprehensive research plan, selected context and sound as the two modalities of interest for memory investigation. I developed behavioral paradigms for mice, aiming to verify whether memory retrieval within a modality leads to memory updating and whether retrieving one modality's memory enhances memory specificity of another modality.
Now I have already validated the behavioral paradigm for sound memory retrieval and demonstrated that retrieving sound modality memory triggers memory updating within the sound modality. Ongoing research involves further experiments and investigations to explore the cross-modal effects of memory retrieval and its underlying circuit mechanisms.
This project was rated as an 'A-level' project under the Academic Research Advancement Program of Tsinghua University, with a grant of $7,000 and initially recommended by the department for the Youth Student Basic Research Program of National Science Foundation

The Function of NRAM+ ensembles in dDG in memory updating

I proficiently utilized the NRAM labeling system to mark and manipulate Npas4-dependent neuronal subpopulations, ensuring accurate targeting of specific neurons for memory updating analysis. I also actively participated in a series of experiments using the contextual fear conditioning (CFC) paradigm in mice, primarily focusing on learning and understanding the research process, Assisted senior researchers in key tasks, such as ensuring the proper maintenance and preparation of the fear conditioning chamber between trials. And I employed optogenetics to inhibit or activate NRAM+ ensembles in mice and subsequently analyzed freezing behavior to validate the necessity and sufficiency of NRAM+ ensembles in memory updating processes.

Upregulation of Raf/MAPK Pathway Underlies Pathophysiology of Mania via Memory Bias

I mastered a series of classic neuroscientific techniques, including: stereotaxic surgeries, perfusion, vibratome sectionin, and confocal imaging. With these techniques, I participated in the research pipeline actively, streamlining processes to guarantee the precision and efficiency of experimental procedures. Meanwhile, I collaborated closely with senior researchers, analyzing and discussing data findings to draw meaningful conclusions and insights.

Diagnosis and Treatment of Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) through Genetic Engineering

I spearheaded the "Mucosal Healing" project, conducted literature reviews to select the appropriate secretion peptides gene, cloned, linked it to TFF3 via a Ser/Gly linker and validated the expression with Western Blotting. To control the expression of TFF3 gene, I introduced the NO sensor as an molecular switch. I also collaborated in editing the introductory video of project, contributed to the iGEM wiki uploads, highlighting the innovative approach for IBD treatment.
